Track Engineering

Built Layer by Layer

Every championship-grade running track is a precisely engineered multilayer system. Each layer plays a specific role in performance, longevity, and athlete safety.

Wearing Course

The topmost surface layer in direct contact with athletes' footwear. Applied as a spray or trowel finish of coloured EPDM granules embedded in PU binder, creating the track's characteristic texture and colour.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the standard size for a running track in Pakistan? +
A standard outdoor running track is 400 metres in circumference with 8 lanes, each 1.22m wide. For IAAF certification, the track must have two 84.39m straight sections and two semi-circular ends with a radius of 36.5m at the inside edge of Lane 1. For schools and recreational use, shorter 200m tracks with 4–6 lanes are commonly installed and are entirely suitable for junior athletics and community sport.
How long does it take to install a full 400m running track? +
A complete 400m track installation typically takes 6–10 weeks from groundbreaking to final handover, depending on sub-base conditions, surface system selected, and weather. The preparatory earthworks and asphalt base take 3–4 weeks, with the PU surface system application requiring a further 7–10 days including curing time. We provide a detailed programme at the design stage so clients can plan accordingly.
What colours are available for running track surfaces? +
Our standard professional track colour is International Red (Brick Red), which is the most common globally and meets IAAF visibility requirements. We also supply tracks in Royal Blue, Forest Green, Tan/Beige, and Graphite. For school and recreational tracks, our EPDM system is available in 12 vibrant standard colours including yellow, orange, blue, and green. Lane markings are always supplied in white for maximum contrast and visibility.
How much does a running track cost in Pakistan? +
Running track costs vary significantly by system type, site conditions, and project scope. As a general guide: a school 200m EPDM track starts from PKR 25–35 lakh for 4 lanes; a collegiate prefabricated 400m track runs from PKR 80–120 lakh; and a full IAAF Class 1 polyurethane 400m track starts from PKR 180–250 lakh. We provide a detailed, itemised quotation at no charge following a free site survey. Contact us for a precise estimate for your project.
How do I maintain a polyurethane running track? +
PU running tracks are very low maintenance. Regular care includes: sweeping or blowing away loose debris weekly, washing with water and mild detergent monthly, and inspecting for any spike damage, cracks, or delamination annually. Avoid using petroleum-based cleaning agents, high-pressure washers above 50 bar, or metallic brushes. Spike length should not exceed 9mm for long jump and throws run-ups (6mm spikes recommended for all track use). With proper care, a quality PU surface will last 20+ years.
Can an existing track be resurfaced rather than completely replaced? +
Yes — in many cases an existing cinder, tarmac, or worn rubber track can be resurfaced rather than rebuilt from scratch, significantly reducing cost and disruption. Our engineers will assess the existing base for flatness, structural integrity, drainage, and contamination. If the base is sound, we can apply a new polyurethane or EPDM surface system directly over it. If the base has extensive cracking, heaving, or drainage problems, a full reconstruction is recommended for long-term performance.
